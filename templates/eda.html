Credit History Significance

Primary Predictor

Applicants with a "Good" credit history record (1.0) have an extremely high approval rate compared to those with poor or no history (0.0). Credit history is mathematically the most significant coefficient in all classification trees.

Income vs Loan Request

Distribution & Correlation

A scatter distribution mapping applicant monthly income against the requested loan amount in thousands. This highlights that while high income often allows for high-value loans, outliers are frequently flagged for manual risk mitigation.

Property Area Outcomes

Demographic Variable

Analysis of loans grouped by Property Area (Rural, Semiurban, Urban). Semiurban properties have a slightly higher likelihood of loan approvals, which could be attributed to lower risk margins or local development incentives.
